Building a Resilient Generation of Adolescent Girls, Young Women, and Adolescent Boys

Empowering 1 Million Youth Across Sierra Leone Over 5 Years

About the Initiative

The Sierra Leone Youth Empowerment Initiative (SLYE) is a five-year national program designed to empower 1 million Adolescent Girls, Young Women (AGYW), and Adolescent Boys (AB) aged 10–24 across all districts of Sierra Leone.

Through education support, life skills training, sexual and reproductive health services, and economic empowerment, SLYE aims to create a resilient, skilled, and socially responsible generation.

Project Objectives

  • Provide life skills and comprehensive sexuality education to 1 million youth.
  • Increase economic opportunities for 500,000 youth.
  • Establish 10,000 Youth Hubs nationwide.
  • Promote gender equality and youth-friendly policies.

Core Activities

Life Skills & Education

Standardized curriculum delivery, mentor training, radio programs, and digital learning platforms.

Economic Empowerment

Vocational training, entrepreneurship grants, digital skills training, job placements and internships.

Youth Hubs

10,000 physical and virtual hubs equipped with learning tools, mentorship programs, and youth-friendly health services.

Advocacy & Policy

National campaigns, youth summits, and collaboration with the Government of Sierra Leone to strengthen youth policy and combat GBV.

Expected Impact

  • Reduction in adolescent pregnancy rates.
  • Increased youth employment and income levels.
  • Growth of youth-led businesses.
  • Improved attitudes toward gender equality.
